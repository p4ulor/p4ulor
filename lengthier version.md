### **Life lessons**

My motto(which is on my profile page) is: 
"Most things, aren't hard, they're just not well explained".
And that's how I will approach my posts. I will try to do my best in:

I learned latter on in life, specially during this university journey, that the motto I wrote above is so true(for example, 3Blue1Brown youtube channel easily proves it).
There were things that I thought I could never comprehend and apply in real life. And I saw others this way, but we eventually can learn what we thought we couldn't.
Something was either wrong or missing. There were a number of things I realised that were the possible cause of hindrance for students (or an any other person) to learn something.

This is what I found:
- I realised that (most of the time) the problem was the form of teaching or the teacher and not the student. Most teachers dont know how to simplify things and tell the whole story. It's that simple
- Sometimes the student must learn by himself, make his own appointments and practice what he learned
- And one of the most imporant aspects: the student must understand why and how something(a concept, a theory, a solution, a machine, etc) came to be. The "history" , if you will, of something and it's pratical use in real life, along with it's nature/beauty(some concepts can be beautiful just because they work and make our life easier). The information you retain the most, is the one you can understand it's usefulness and the one you personally make use of it
- And finally, the student must cut back on other hobbies and have a healthy lifestyle(good diet and sleep per example)

Also, what really hits be the hardest with knowledge is books, articles and youtube videos(the internet in general). I think that these 3 sources are often more reliable, efficient and are easier to learn things than many teachers.
One reason as to why I think this way of spreading knowledge and teaching is better is because it's prepared and ready to transmit information. It's more dedicated and more elaborate. A book shouldn't leave questions open, because you can't contact the author...
But it looks like some teachers leave questions open because they wan't to make the students come to them, they want to be useful. Or because some teachers:

- Provide poor material because they have limited time to teach. Thus, they want to make things seem shorter or simpler than what they actually are, but that only hurts the students...
- Aren't ready to spew all the topics and details in a class. To be honest, it's hard and some just don't get ready for classes
- They provide and design powerpoints appointments in such a way(minimalistic) that you have to watch their classes in order to understand the subject
But don't me wrong, teachers can be useful when you want to receive a quick answer to something, or just by aspect of having a healthy face to face interaction with someone else. 

I just think that we(mainly university students) have to stop putting so much trust and dependency in SOME teachers since they can teach some things wrong or not efficiently. And begin relying on ourselves. And I'm specially talking about teachers from universities. Most of these are like "ah, who cares, my students will just learn by themselves(they don't have much of a choice) I don't need to try so hard to teach or provide studying material".
Think about it, you often easily learn skills by yourself. You don't want the teachers to do the thinking and discovery for you, to some degree.
Another factor that comes into play, is that the teachers are tired of teaching. Imagine teaching the same class for 2 semesters per year. Always the same thing over and over again. You begin to drop of pieces of information, get bored with it and speed up. And most importantly, you begin to lack the perpective of what it's like to learn what you're teaching to someone else that doesn't know anything about what you're teaching.
I'm not trashing teachers, it's just that in MY opinion, some students that are having hardships should think about being their own teachers and rethink on how they learn.
To sum up this topic, you just have to analyise and gain experience with teachers to understand if they're good or not(there's a few of them that are). Because when they are not, chances are you are better of teaching yourself from the sources I mentioned and just help yourself. Or maybe I'm too harsh, resentful or picky on teachers? I rather doubt it. It may just be my experience.
